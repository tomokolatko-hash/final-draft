Fish in Sea

​Exporting 3D designs from Adobe Illustrator, animating them in Blender, and then editing in Premiere turned out to be more difficult than I had expected. At every step, I had to make sure the files were in the correct formats otherwise, nothing would work properly. Without your demo videos, I wouldn’t have been able to complete this assignment. Thank you so much for providing them. I added more objects and recreated “The Fish Under the Sea” with motion. I also included two additional artworks that are completely different from my initial submission. For the bear, I used a few simple shapes available in Blender, and I added an ice cream and a hat to give some motion to the scene. I used Photoshop to add a background image of a café. Additionally, I created another image using OBJ files downloaded from TurboSquid. I just wanted to experiment with it. I thought the movie my classmate made of a car driving around a mansion was really cool and inspiring. When rendering, the colors didn’t appear the same as they did in Illustrator, so I adjusted the scene by adding new lighting, including a Sun light. Although I struggled to achieve the exact colors I originally envisioned, I experimented with different options. Instead of going for realistic tones, I intentionally chose soft pastel colors for both the fish and the bear. At first, I had no idea how the camera worked or how to set the right angles, but I think I understand it much better now. :) Throughout this assignment, I learned several new things like how to properly export files as OBJs and how to think more carefully about color selection. It ended up being a really valuable learning experience. I really enjoyed seeing my objects come to life through motion. It was a fun and creative process!
